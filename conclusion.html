Conclusion

Chester Square Neighbors seeked to better understand public transportation habits near their neighborhood. By combining several datasets provided by the MBTA, our group was able to create a visualization that offers insights into how often people use the relevant public transportation stations, and when during the day they use them.

We discovered that the rail stations (Symphony station on the Green line and Mass Ave station on the Orange line) are used much more often than any of the bus stops. We also learned that "PM Peak" between 4pm and 6:29pm is the busiest time to take public transportation.

Through our exploratory data analysis, we discovered that bikes are increasing in number while cars are decreasing in number in the area, which should encourage the addition of safe bike lanes. Accidents occur most frequently at the intersections of Melnea Cass and Mass Ave, and Albany St and Mass Ave. We also learned that the MBTA has become more reliable over the past five years.

Overall, we hope that we have been able to offer Chester Square Neighbors some insights into public transportation usage in order to contribute to the advocacy for continued infrastructure investment in the area.
